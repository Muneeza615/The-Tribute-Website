OUR NATIONAL HERO

Dr.Abdul Qadeer Khan

1936-2021

Father of Pakistan's Nuclear Program

Abdul Qadeer Khan (born April 1, 1936, Bhopal, India—died October 10, 2021, Islamabad, Pakistan) was a Pakistani engineer, a key figure in Pakistan’s nuclear weapons program who was also involved for decades in a black market of nuclear technology and know-how whereby uranium-enrichment centrifuges, nuclear warhead designs, missiles, and expertise were sold or traded to Iran, North Korea, Libya, and possibly other countries.In 1947, during Khan’s childhood, India achieved independence from Britain, and Muslim areas in the east and west were partitioned to form the state of Pakistan. Khan immigrated to West Pakistan in 1952, and in 1960 he graduated from the University of Karachi with a degree in metallurgy. Over the next decade he pursued graduate studies abroad, first in West Berlin and then in Delft, Netherlands, where in 1967 he received a master’s degree in metallurgy.

Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan's most famous quote regarding Pakistan's nuclear capability is:


"Nation should be rest assured Pakistan is a safe atomic power. No one can cast an evil eye on it"

Biographies

  • Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan was born on 1 April 1936 in Bhopal, India.
  • After the partition of 1947, he migrated to Pakistan with his family.
  • He was a Pakistani nuclear scientist and metallurgical engineer.
  • Dr. A.Q. Khan studied in Germany, the Netherlands, and Belgium, earning higher degrees in engineering.
  • He played a key role in Pakistan’s nuclear program, which aimed to strengthen national defense.
  • He founded the Kahuta Research Laboratories (KRL) in 1976.
  • Because of his services, he is often called the “Father of Pakistan’s Nuclear Program.”
  • Dr. A.Q. Khan received several national awards, including Nishan-e-Imtiaz.
  • Apart from science, he also wrote articles and supported education and research in Pakistan.
  • Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan passed away on 10 October 2021 in Islamabad, Pakistan.
